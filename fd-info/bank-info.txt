**Types of Banks**
The banking sector is divided into various specialized institutions that cater to different financial needs:
*   **Retail and Commercial Banks:** Retail banks deal directly with individuals and small businesses to provide everyday services like savings accounts, personal loans, and credit cards. Commercial banks typically refer to divisions that handle deposits and larger loans for corporations and mid-market businesses. 
*   **Investment Banks:** Instead of taking traditional deposits, these institutions focus on capital markets. They help corporations and governments raise funds by underwriting stock and bond issues, advising on mergers and acquisitions (M&A), and trading securities.
*   **Universal Banks:** These are massive, diversified financial services companies that combine multiple activities, often distributing insurance alongside banking products in a model known as bancassurance.
*   **Central Banks:** Government-owned entities that supervise commercial banks, control cash interest rates, provide liquidity to the financial system, and act as the lender of last resort during economic crises.
*   **Specialized and Niche Banks:** This includes **Credit Unions and Cooperative Banks** (not-for-profit institutions owned by their depositors), **Private Banks** (which manage wealth for high-net-worth individuals), **Direct or Internet-only Banks** (which operate entirely online without physical branches), and **Islamic Banks** (which adhere to Islamic law by avoiding interest and earning profit through markups).

**Fixed Deposit (FD) Schemes**
A Fixed Deposit is a financial instrument where money is locked in for a predetermined period at a fixed interest rate, allowing investors to generate predictable, compounding wealth over time. To cater to different financial goals, the market offers several variations of the standard FD:
*   **Senior Citizen FDs:** These schemes typically offer a higher interest rate premium specifically for older individuals.
*   **Tax-Saving 5-Year FDs:** These allow investors to claim income tax deductions, though the funds are strictly locked in for a five-year tenure.
*   **Monthly Income FD Schemes:** Instead of paying out the principal and compounded interest entirely at maturity, these schemes provide regular monthly interest payouts to act as a steady income stream.
*   **Company Fixed Deposits:** These are term deposits offered by financial and non-financial corporations rather than traditional banks. 
*   **Digital FDs:** Modern fintech infrastructure has made booking FDs frictionless. For example, users can now open an FD via platforms like Google Pay (in partnership with institutions like Equitas Small Finance Bank) in just a few minutes, entirely bypassing the need to open a traditional savings account with that specific bank.

**Current World Scenario in Finance and Economics**
The global economic landscape in 2026 is currently characterized by a mix of technological advancement, geopolitical volatility, and debt pressures:
*   **Geopolitical Fragmentation and Inflation:** The global economy is being severely tested by geopolitical stand-offs, including the conflict in the Middle East, trade frictions between the US and China, and the ongoing war in Ukraine. Disruptions to shipping routes, such as the Strait of Hormuz, have caused energy and commodity prices to surge. This supply shock raises costs globally, threatening to prolong inflationary pressures well into 2026. 
*   **The Global Debt Trap:** In their fight against inflation, Western central banks have pushed interest rates to multi-decade highs. This has created a massive "yield vacuum" that is sucking capital out of emerging markets and into safe-haven assets like US Treasuries. Consequently, low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) are sliding into a sovereign debt catastrophe, forced to issue short-term debt at punishing double-digit yields simply to roll over their existing obligations. 
*   **The Rise of Shadow Banking:** The non-bank financial system (NBFS)—often called shadow banking—has boomed to an estimated $238.8 trillion, representing nearly 49% of total global financial assets. Private credit funds and leveraged loan markets are expanding rapidly outside the traditional regulatory perimeter, raising systemic stability concerns reminiscent of the imbalances seen before the 2008 financial crisis.
*   **Investment Banking and AI Integration:** Despite macroeconomic headwinds, capital markets are seeing a resurgence in IPO momentum and private equity exits. Furthermore, investment banks are moving past experimental phases and robustly integrating Generative AI into their core operations, utilizing AI agents to automate trade accounting, client onboarding, and pitch book preparation.
